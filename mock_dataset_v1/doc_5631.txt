No agreement reached on the reform of Working Time Directive after conciliation The working time directive limits the working week to 48 hours averaged out over a 17-week period, but it allows member states to opt out from that provision. It also regulates resting time and on-call time. After years of negotiations to reform the directive, the European Parliament and Council could not find a compromise on three crucial points: the opt-out, on-call time and multiple contracts.
