11 October 2000 MOTION FOR A RESOLUTION pursuant to Rule 48 of the Rules of Procedure by Bart Staes, Gorka Knörr Borràs, Jillian Evans, Nelly Maes, Carlos Bautista Ojeda, Ian Stewart Hudghton, Camilo Nogueira Román, Donald Neil MacCormick, Josu Ortuondo Larrea and Eurig Wyn on the infringement of the right to freedom of opinion and human rights in Italy Motion for a European Parliament resolution on the infringement of the right to freedom of opinion and human rights in Italy The European Parliament, A. whereas Dr Giuseppe Segato, who was born in Borgoricco (province of Padua) on 17 June 1954, has been sentenced to three years and seven months in prison for 'subversion' for giving moral support to the group that - without resorting to violence – occupied the bell-tower in Piazza San Marco in Venice on 9 May 1997 in order to call for self-rule for the Veneto, B. whereas there are still laws in force in Italy, such as Article 241 of the Criminal Code, which were passed during the Fascist dictatorship of Benito Mussolini and under which anyone who seeks to promote by political and peaceful means the independence of part of the existing territory of the Republic of Italy can be sent to prison, 1. Questions whether the behaviour of the Italian judiciary in this and other cases does not openly infringe the right to freedom of opinion, which forms the basis of every democracy and of Article 6 of the Treaty on European Union; 2. Also questions whether the provisions of the Italian 'Codice Rocco' (the Criminal Code of 1930), particularly those aimed at so-called crimes of opinion and persons endangering the 'unity of the state', which were laid down during the Fascist dictatorship of Benito Mussolini and are still in force, do not infringe the principles of liberty, democracy, and respect for human rights, fundamental freedoms and the state of law enshrined in the Treaty.
