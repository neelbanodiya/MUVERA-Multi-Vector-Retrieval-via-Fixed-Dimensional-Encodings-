Question for written answer E-001437/2011 to the Commission Rule 117 William (The Earl of) Dartmouth (EFD) (17 February 2011) Subject: Understanding English Does the Commission, in the context of its reference to the 12 % of VAT uncollected owing to ‘… evasion, avoidance …’ on page 16 of COM(2010)0608 Towards a Single Market Act For a highly competitive social market economy 50 proposals for improving our work, business and exchanges with one another, appreciate that in the English language at least, the difference between ‘evasion’ and ‘avoidance’ is that the former is illegal and the latter legal, and thus it is by definition impossible to prevent avoidance because the very word means in this context that the activity is permitted by the law?
