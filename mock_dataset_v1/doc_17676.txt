Arthur, Prime Minister and Minister of Finance of Barbados, who officially opened the Assembly, said, "Half the world is represented here and the other half is listening." He reminded delegates that the Parliament of Barbados was the second oldest Parliament in the Commonwealth. He described the EU-ACP Joint Parliamentary Assembly as the greatest example of North-South cooperation known to humanity and said it had successfully managed to incorporate new EU and ACP members. On the EPAs, Prime Minister Arthur underlined the reciprocal nature of any agreement, the respect for human rights as an essential element of any future agreement and the importance of regional cooperation.
