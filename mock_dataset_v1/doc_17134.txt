Welcomes the IGAD initiative to organise the Nairobi Somali reconciliation conference, welcomes the decision of the Transitional National Government of Somalia to participate in this conference and urges all other Somali parties to do likewise without preconditions and with a genuine resolve to broaden and complete the national reconciliation process; 4. Calls on the EU to examine ways and means of supporting IGAD's efforts to assist the Somalis to achieve peace and national reconciliation and to put an end to a situation which is causing much distress and suffering among the civilian population; 5. Calls on the EU to assist the Somali Transitional National Government in establishing its authority throughout Somalia, in particular by contributing to the UNDP Somali Civil Protection Programme, which is aimed at improving standards and practices within the judiciary, promoting law enforcement, supporting civil society, effecting the demobilisation of armed militia groups, and enhancing landmine action; 6. Calls on the EU, in close cooperation with UN agencies, to step up its humanitarian assistance to refugees and displaced persons and to implement aid and rehabilitation programmes through the intermediary of NGOs and local civil society organisations; 7. Urges strict application of the arms embargo against Somalia imposed by UN Security Council Resolutions 733, 751 and 1356; 8. Instructs its President to forward this resolution to the Council, the Commission, IGAD, the OAU and the Transitional National Government of Somalia.
