A pre-session press conference will be held in the PHS building, Room 0A50, on the Friday before the part session at 11 a.m. A final briefing will be held at 4.30 p.m. on Monday, 16 December 2002 in the Press Room in Strasbourg. Council & Commission Statements Outcome of the Copenhagen Summit European Council report and Commission statement on the Copenhagen European Council meeting – 12/13 December 2002 Debate : 18.12.2002 The outcome of the crucial summit will be analysed on Wednesday morning. The Commission and the Council will be making statements on the deal over future EU financing including structural funds and direct subsidies within the Common Agricultural Policy reached with the first ten enlargement countries. The Commission’s assessment of Turkey’s progress will also be analysed. Assessment of the Danish Presidency – Enlargement, Enlargement, Enlargement Statement by t he President-in-Office of the Council on the work of the Danish presidency Debate : 18.12.2002 The main priority of the Danish Presidency has been enlargement. Other priorities include: freedom, security and justice, sustainable development, food safety and global responsibility. The Council will make a statement assessing the Danish Presidency. The Presidency will pass to Greece in January 2003 and Italy for the second half of 2003. Moves Towards a Common Asylum and Immigration Policy Counci l and Commission statements on immigration and asylum policy Debate : 18.12.2002 The Council and the Commission will be making statements on recent progress in immigration and asylum policy. As the EU works towards a common immigration and asylum policy, the statements are likely to focus on a debate on voluntary repatriation agreements, as the European Parliament was not properly consulted before the first such agreement was signed with Hong Kong.
