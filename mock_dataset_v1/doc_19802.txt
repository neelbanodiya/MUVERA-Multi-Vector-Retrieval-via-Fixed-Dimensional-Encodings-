Question for written answer E-003447/2011 to the Commission Rule 117 Georgios Papanikolaou (PPE) (11 April 2011) Subject: European citizens' lack of awareness of the EU Charter of Fundamental Rights According to a survey by the European Ombudsman, 72 % of European citizens believe they are not adequately informed regarding the rights safeguarded by the EU Charter of Fundamental Rights, while 13 % of them do not even know of the Charter's existence. Furthermore, 42 % of those taking part in the survey said they were dissatisfied with the degree of transparency in the European administration. In what way does the Commission intend to raise the awareness of European citizens regarding the rights provided by the EU Charter of Fundamental Rights? What steps is the Commission intending to take to increase transparency, citizens' confidence and their participation in the decision-making process in view of the fact that the Lisbon Treaty and the EU Charter of Fundamental Rights were enacted with the aim of bringing citizens closer to the EU?
