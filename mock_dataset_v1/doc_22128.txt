He admitted that certain states did not know how to resist such pressure. In his response, Mr Kirsch stated that as a judge it was difficult to answer sensitive political questions. However, he recalled that the states did not have to conclude bilateral agreements in contradiction with their obligations resulting from the Statute. This was essentially a political question, stated Mr Kirsch, who considered that it was not for the Court to try and find a solution to this problem. Moreover, Article 98 of the Court Statute authorises states to respect the obligations which are incumbent on them in terms of immunity. It was possible, he said, for one of these bilateral agreements to be subjected to examination by the Court in terms of compatibility with Article 98 of the ICC. For this reason, Mr Kirsch did not publicly declare his point of view on this subject. Mr Kirsch also did not want to declare his position on the questions from Johan VAN HECKE (ALDE, BE) regarding the pressure exercised by the international community to finalise peace processes. He gave the example of the situation in Uganda and in the DRC.
