In its turn, the Codex Alimentarious Commission, which was created in 1963 by the FAO and the World Health Organisation (WHO) and is an international reference body that reports to the United Nations and the WHO, adopted a standard on fermented milk at its meeting in Rome at the end of June 2003 that makes it impossible for desserts pasteurised after fermentation to be denominated yoghurts , at least in countries without any legislation governing this area.
