The Commission's policy in the field of social policy is based on a broad, comprehensive approach, which is set out in the renewed Social Agenda. Under that overall approach, different roles are played by the various instruments, which range from legal provisions and the Open Method of Coordination to financial instruments, such as the ESF and Progress, which make up an important strand of EU social policy. Such financial instruments should not, however, be regarded as the only instruments relevant to social policy: indeed instruments of all types are used as part of a coherent overall package. The ESF is clearly the most important financial instrument at EU level for promoting employment and social cohesion in the Member States. It is used in close coordination with other policies, such as the Open Method of Coordination in the field of social protection and social inclusion.
