Question for written answer E-003551/2011 to the Commission Rule 117 Ashley Fox (ECR) (11 April 2011) Subject: The EU and human rights in Equatorial Guinea — imprisonment of Gerardo Angüe Mangue, Cruz Obiang Ebele, Juan Ecomo Ndong, Emiliano Esono Michá and Gumersindo Ramirez Faustino Gerardo Angüe Mangue, Cruz Obiang Ebele, Juan Ecomo Ndong, Emiliano Esono Michá and Gumersindo Ramirez Faustino are imprisoned in Equatorial Guinea on alleged politically motivated charges. According to Amnesty International, the five men are former members of the Progress Party for Equatorial Guinea who were arrested without a warrant and convicted of possessing arms and ammunition despite no evidence being produced in court. There are also some serious concerns about their treatment in prison. What is the status of these prisoners, and what is the Commission doing to ensure that their human rights are respected? Moreover, what is the Commission doing to promote human rights in Equatorial Guinea?
