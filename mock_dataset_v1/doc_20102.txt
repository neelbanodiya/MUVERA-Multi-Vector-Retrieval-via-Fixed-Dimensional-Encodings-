This would appear to be the right time to do so, since we are in a crucial period during which there is a legal mandate for the redefinition of Community fisheries policy in line with the current situation, which is quite different from the situation in the 1990s. The industry expects and has a right to demand that those responsible in both national and Community administrations should be capable of coping with the circumstances and challenges facing us and should be able to guarantee the viability and future prospects of an activity which is as old as history itself and which represents a factor of economic and social cohesion in Europe.
