Press enquiries: Maria Andrés Marìn (Strasbourg) tel.(33-3) 881 73603 (Brussels) tel.(32-2) 28 44299 e-mail : agri-press@europarl.eu.int European Council Call for relaunch of IGC Motions for resolutions o n the preparation of the European Council on 25-26 March 2004 Doc.: B5-0118/2004 , B5-0119/2004 , B5-0120/2004 Debate: 10.03.2004 Vote: 11.03.2004 Vote Parliament has adopted a resolution on the forthcoming EU summit on 25-26 March and the Intergovernmental Conference. MEPs call on every member of the European Council to show the flexibility that is inevitably needed to avoid deadlock at the IGC, and warn it is unlikely that delay will make agreement on the remaining issues easier. They call for the European Council to launch an immediate resumption of the IGC, to reach a conclusion before 1 May 2004 on the basis of the draft constitutional treaty put forward by the Convention, without changing its fundamental balance, which some proposals risk doing. Parliament warns that the voting system of the Treaty of Nice, which comes into force on 1 November, will make decision making in the Council much more difficult than it is now. MEPs call for the Irish Presidency to take all the necessary steps to break the current deadlock. Finally, Parliament expresses its belief that failure to reach a constitutional settlement would frustrate the integration process, render the Union unable to contemplate any further enlargement and result in a devastating loss of solidarity and legitimacy.
