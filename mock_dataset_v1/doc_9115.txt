The internet is a source of danger, but we should not get hung up on the "schizophrenia" of security, speakers said. While Internet criminality (such as identity theft) needs to be fought effectively, the right to access information must be maintained. Given the growing risk of e-illiteracy - "the new illiteracy of 21st Century"- cutting somebody's internet access would not be a proportionate measure, he said. "Freedom needs to be regulated, yet we want to let the internet thrive", said Professor Stefano Rodotà from the EU's Fundamental Rights Agency, who is concerned about the "digital divide". The Lambrinidis' report, is due to be discussed by the whole parliament in the week of 23 March.
