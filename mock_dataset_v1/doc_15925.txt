Question for written answer E-012527/2011 to the Commission Rule 117 Kay Swinburne (ECR) (10 January 2012) Subject: European Court of Auditors For 17 consecutive years, the European Court of Auditors has found grave errors in payments in the EU’s accounts, amounting to some EUR 4.6 billion, as a result of which it has refused to sign off on them. It has cited a 3.7 % error rate for payments in fields including agriculture, foreign aid and development. Can the Commission clarify: — How it will ensure that payments made by Member States are more accurate in the future? — What measures can be put in place to ensure better compliance?
