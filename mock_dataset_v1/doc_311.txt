REACH would replace over 40 existing directives and regulations. It comes under the codecision procedure, and both the legislative institutions - Parliament and Council - are currently working on it. Parliament's first reading is expected in early autumn. Under REACH, companies that manufacture or import more than one tonne of a chemical substance per year would have to register it in a central database. The companies would have to assess the risks arising from use of the chemical and take measures to manage any such risks. This would reverse the burden of proof, from public authorities to industry, for ensuring the safety of chemicals on the market.
