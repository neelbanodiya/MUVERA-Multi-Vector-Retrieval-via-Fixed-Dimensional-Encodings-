Answer given by Mr Piebalgs on behalf of the Commission (16 June 2011) 1. The Commission provides grants for co-financing to non-state actors specifically through its Non-State actors Programmes For more details see http://ec.europa.eu/europeaid/what/civil-society/index_en.htm . These grants are attributed on the basis of calls for proposals that are regularly published on the Commission's DG EuropeAid Development and Cooperation website http://ec.europa.eu/europeaid/work/ap/index_en.htm . The European Commission has recently designed a web-based tool to assist civil society partners obtain all the relevant information when seeking a grant https://webgate.ec.europa.eu/fpfis/mwikis/aidco/index.php/Main_Page . All potential beneficiaries must also be registered, when applying for a grant, in the Commission database PADOR. Registration is to be carried out by the organisations through the Pador link on website address mentioned below. 2. The Commission supports health in developing countries through various channels, in particular through — specific bilateral health sector support programmes to countries where health has been identified as a particular cooperation sector, — budget support of which the variable element is linked to performance in the health sector (as is for example the case in Tanzania), — its direct contributions to global health initiatives and to specialised United Nations (UN) agencies — and last but not least through its thematic and non-state actors' projects grant support, that is attributed on a competitive basis via calls for proposals as mentioned before. 3. The health situation in Tanzania is regularly monitored through the Government of Tanzania, in collaboration with its international partners and national stakeholders (with a strong presence of faith-based service providers), in the context of the Joint Annual Health Reviews http://www.tzdpg.or.tz/dpghealth/ .
