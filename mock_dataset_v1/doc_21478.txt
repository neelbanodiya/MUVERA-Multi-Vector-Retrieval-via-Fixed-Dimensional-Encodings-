Answer given by Mr Kyprianou on behalf of the Commission (30 August 2007) The Commission is not aware of the existence of any European fund to meet costs of hospital treatment obtained in another Member State as the result of an accident as mentioned by the Honourable Member. However, in the framework of the regulations on coordination of social security schemes Council Regulation (EC) No 1408/71 of 14 June 1971 on the application of social security schemes to employed persons, to selfâ€‘employed persons and to members of their families moving within the Community, OJ L 149, 5.7.1971. Regulation lastly amended by Regulation (EC) No 1992/2006 of Parliament and of the Council of 18.12.2006, OJ L 392, 30.12.2006 and Council Regulation (EC) No 574/72 laying down the procedure for implementing Regulation (EEC) No 1408/71 on the application of social security schemes to employed persons, to self employed persons, to self-employed persons and to their families moving within the Community, OJ L 74, 27.3.1972. Regulation lastly amended by Commission Regulation (EC) No 207/2006 of 7 February 2006, OJ L 36, 8.2.2006. , patients who are covered by the statutory sickness insurance of a Member State and whose treatment becomes necessary during a stay in the territory of another Member State, for example as the result of an accident, are entitled to benefits as provided for by the legislation of the Member State of stay and at the expense of the Member State of residence (Article 22 of Council Regulation (EC) No 1408/71). These entitlements are certified by the European Health Insurance Card which is used in all Member States of the European Economic Area and in Switzerland as from 1 January 2006.
