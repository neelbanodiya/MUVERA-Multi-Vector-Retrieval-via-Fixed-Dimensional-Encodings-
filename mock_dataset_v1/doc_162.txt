Question for written answer E-004535/2011 to the Commission Rule 117 Andreas Mölzer (NI) (10 May 2011) Subject: Counterfeit euro coins According to a report in the Austrian daily newspaper Österreich , counterfeit euro coins have recently appeared in Austria for the first time. Most of them appear to be 2-euro coins. After the multi-million euro scam in Germany at the end of March — a criminal gang had ordered 29 million tonnes of counterfeit coins from Asia — numerous counterfeit coins now appear to have found their way into Austria as well. Thousands of counterfeit coins are in circulation in Austria every year. In 2010 the Austrian Mint, as the competent institution, discovered a total of 10 027 counterfeit coins. 1. What is the situation EU‑wide? Is it possible to say whether the number of counterfeit euro coins in circulation is increasing? 2. What is the source of most of these counterfeit coins? 3. Are there any EU‑wide estimates as to the value of the counterfeit euro coins? 4. Is any transnational action being taken to prevent the distribution of counterfeit coins? 5. If so, what action is being taken? 6. If so, which European institution is responsible for its coordination? 7. Is it possible to say which EU Member State has the largest number of counterfeit coins in circulation? 8. Which coins are most susceptible to counterfeiting?
