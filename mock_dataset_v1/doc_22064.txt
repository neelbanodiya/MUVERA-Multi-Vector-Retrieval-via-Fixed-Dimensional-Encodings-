Joint answer given by Mr Potočnik on behalf of the Commission Written questions : E-004943/11 , E-004944/11 (27 July 2011) The Commission confirms that the Kingdom of Spain is being referred to Court for its failure to adopt and communicate the river basin management plans required under Directive 2000/60 Directive 2000/60/EC of the European Parliament and of the Council of 23 October 2000 establishing a framework for Community action in the field of water policy, OJ L 327, 22.12.2000. in several of its river basin districts (RBD). In particular, given that Spain has failed to fulfil its obligations under Article 13(1), (2), (3) and (6) (except for the RBD of ‘Distrito de Cuenca Fluvial de Cataluña),’ Article 14 (1)(c) (except for the RBD of ‘Islas Baleares; Guadalquivir; Cuenca Mediterránea Andaluza; Tinto-Odiel-Piedras; Guadalete-Barbate; Tenerife; Galicia-Costa; Miño-Sil; Duero; Cantábrico Occidental; Cantábrico Oriental and Guadiana’) and Article 15(1) (except for the RBD of ‘Distrito de Cuenca Fluvial de Cataluña)’ of Directive 2000/60, on 16 June 2011 the Commission has decided to refer Spain to the ECJ. At this stage of the infringement procedure, the Commission has no further information to share, with regard to the questions raised by the Honourable Member.
