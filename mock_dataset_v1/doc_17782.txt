Denounces the completely disproportionate actions of violence by the Russian authorities in Chechnya and the gross violations of human rights against the civilian population as well as the violation of international humanitarian law, in particular of the Geneva Convention on Refugees, resulting from these actions; 2. Calls upon the Russian authorities to bring an immediate halt to its military attack against the people of Chechnya and to resume the political dialogue asked for by Chechen President Maskhadov on the urgent questions of internal security and economic reconstruction and cooperation on the basis of the peace agreement of 1996; 3. Calls upon the Council to protest in the strongest terms to the Russian Government and to suspend the implementation of the Partnership and Cooperation Agreement between the Russian Federation and the European Union until the military attack and gross violations of human rights against the people of Chechnya have come to an end; 4. Urges the Council and in particular the High Representative for the CFSP, Mr Javier Solana, to make efforts to place the Chechen crisis at the top of the agenda of the next OSCE Summit on 18-19 November 1999 in Istanbul; 5. Urges the Council to develop as soon as possible a Common Strategy for the Caucasus; 6. Points out that the Russian Federation has made a commitment to the Council of Europe to respect human rights on its territory and not to use its army against minorities living in the Federation; invites therefore the Council of Europe to examine the situation in Chechnya in the light of these obligations; 7. Calls for the constitution of a regional roundtable within the framework of the Black Sea Cooperation Council bringing together the governments of the Russian Federation, the neighbouring Republics of the Russian Federation, and the member states of the Black Sea Council with the aim of promoting security and economic regional integration; 8.
