25/10/2004 MOTION FOR A RESOLUTION Tabled by Mrs Muscardini Pursuant to Rule 115 of the Rules of Procedure On behalf of the UEN Group on Explosive Remnants of War and Anti Personnel Landmines The European Parliament , - having regard to Protocol V to the UN Convention on Certain Conventional Weapons on Explosive Remnants of War (ERW) of 28 November 2003; - having regard to the Ottawa Convention on the Prohibition of the Use, Stockpiling, Production and Transfer of Anti-Personnel Mines (APL) and on Their Destruction of 1997; A. recognising that most EU Member States have signed and ratified the Ottawa Convention to globally ban anti-personnel landmines, and hence do not use these types of weaponry any longer; B. whereas the armed forces of the democracies make special effort in their military operations to minimise collateral damage and do not deliberately target civilians; 1. Recognises that ERW may kill and injure civilians long after conflicts have ended and may obstruct the delivery of humanitarian assistance, prevent the cultivation of crops and slow the reconstruction and rehabilitation of war-affected areas; 2. Congratulates the Commission and Member States on the support they have provided for global mine action and encourages both to intensify their efforts in order to end the threat to civilians from ERW and APL by 2010 at the latest; 5. Urges all states which are not party to Protocol V on ERW and the Ottawa Convention on APL to sign and ratify both and to keep to their spirit in the interim period; 6. Underlines the responsibility of a state in control of territory to provide warnings, education and other measures to protect the civilian population from ERW under the provisions of the Protocol; 7. Calls on the Commission to increase funding for assistance to communities and individuals affected by unexploded cluster munitions through all available instruments; 8. Instructs its President to forward this resolution to the Commission, the Council, the governments of Member States, the UN Secretary-General, the International Committee of the Red Cross, and all States Parties to the Convention.
