The capital of the EBRD was doubled to €20 billion in 1997, its brief is to contribute towards developing a market economy in the former Communist States, based on sound banking management and 60% of its investments are directed towards the private sector. Mr Markov appreciates the EBRD’s willingness to take risks and to back projects normally considered too risky by private banks. For the future, however, the committee would like the Bank to increase funding for local authorities with a view to improving basic services and develop technical innovation relevant to industry. Supporting alternative energy projects is one way of helping East European States to diversify from nuclear energy. Other points raised in the draft resolution include the absence of a solid based tax system in the emerging countries and human rights, where the committee supports freezing backing for major investment programmes in countries where the human rights situation is worrying but at the same time maintaining small scale financial assistance for independent SMEs. Labour Cost Statistics and Inflation Miquel MAYOL I RAYNAL (Greens/EFA, E) Report on the Council common position with a view to the adoption of a Regulation of the European Parliament and of the Council concerning the labour cost index (10803/2/2002 – C5-0453/2002 – 2001/0166(COD)) Doc.: A5-0420/2002 Procedure : Codecision procedure (2nd reading) Debate : 18.12.2002 Reporting for the Economic and Monetary Affairs Committee, Miquel MAYOL I RAYNAL (Greens/EFA, E) will be recommending approval of a Council common position, unamended on a Commission proposal to improve statistical gathering in the area of labour costs – an important indicator contributing to the measurement of inflation. For the future, however, he would like to see a comparable labour productivity index established. Council has nevertheless, changed implementing comitology procedures from a ”management” committee to a ”regulatory” committee, thus giving more weight to Council in the decision making procedure.
