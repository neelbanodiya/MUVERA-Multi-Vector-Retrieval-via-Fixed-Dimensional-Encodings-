In addition to the Community Civil Protection Mechanism, the Commission has effective specialised mechanisms for the delivery of humanitarian assistance (ECHO). Both ECHO and the Mechanism have, in their respective fields of competence, specific systems (e.g. the 14 points systems The 14 point system of reporting was established several years ago in ECHO and is intended to record all humanitarian contributions (ie humanitarian financing decisions actually taken, NOT pledges) of the Member States and Commission (principally ECHO) in respect of a particular humanitarian emergency. and the Common Emergency Communication and Information System –CECIS CECIS provides a dedicated communication and information system interlinking the national contact points in the Member States. ), which allow close exchange of information and co-operation with Member States. These instruments will continue to be strengthened and provide the backbone of the Community’s immediate response to disaster, particularly in the light of the experience gained in responding to the Tsunami in South East Asia.
