Answer given by Mrs Ferrero-Waldner on behalf of the Commission (12 July 2006) Part of the funding for the construction of the centres in Tangiers and Marrakech is being provided under the Aeneas programme of the European Union. The project, which has a much wider scope than the construction of these centres, has a budget of total eligible costs of EUR 3104978 out of which 64.41 %, that is (EUR 1999999 ) will be funded by the Aeneas programme. This programme gives financial and technical assistance to third countries to better manage migration flows. Projects are selected through calls for proposals. The objectives of the programme, as laid down in Article 2(1)(a) of Regulation (EC) No 491/2004 of the Parliament and the Council of 10 March 2004 Regulation (EC) No 491/2004 of the Parliament and of the Council of 10 March 2004 establishing a programme for financial and technical assistance to third countries in the areas of migration and asylum (AENEAS), OJ L 80, 18.3.2004. are the following: (a) the development of third countries legislation in the field of legal immigration, (b) the development of legal migration, (c) the development of their legislation and national practices as regards international protection, (d) the establishment in the third countries concerned, of an effective and preventive policy in the fight against illegal migration, including the fight against trafficking in human beings and smuggling of migrants, (e) the readmission, in full respect of the law, and durable reintegration, into the third country concerned of persons who have illegally entered or remained on the territory of Member States or of persons who have unsuccessfully applied for asylum in the European Union or benefited from international protection. The project mentioned was presented to the Aeneas Management Committee, composed of delegations of all 25 Member States, on 3 October 2005. Following the favourable opinion delivered by the Committee, the measure was put into the register/repository and the Parliament exercised its right of scrutiny during a one month period.
