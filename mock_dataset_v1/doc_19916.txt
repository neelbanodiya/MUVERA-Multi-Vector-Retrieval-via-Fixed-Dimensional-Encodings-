27 September 2001 MOTION FOR A RESOLUTION further to the Council and Commission statements pursuant to Rule 37(2) of the Rules of Procedure by Fodé Sylla, Giuseppe Di Lello Finuoli and Pernille Frahm on behalf of the GUE/NGL Group on the conclusions of the World Conference against Racism held in Durban European Parliament resolution on the conclusions of the World Conference against Racism held in Durban The European Parliament, – having regard to Articles 6, 7 and 29 of the Treaty on European Union and Article 13 of the Treaty establishing the European Community, – having regard to the relevant international legal instruments to which the Member States are signatories, including the International Convention of 1965 on the Elimination of All Forms of Racial Discrimination, the Framework Convention of 1995 for the Protection of National Minorities and the European Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms, – having regard to Council Directive 2000/43/EC of 29 June 2000 implementing the principle of equal treatment between persons irrespective of racial or ethnic origin, and to the progress made towards the acceptance of diversity in Europe, – having regard to its earlier resolutions on combating racism, xenophobia and anti-Semitism, – having regard to the final declaration and the programme of action adopted in Durban on 8 September by the World Conference against Racism, A. whereas, despite the efforts made at world level and within the European Union, acts of racist violence against immigrants and minorities are on the increase in Europe and around the world, B. whereas the Member States' colonial past is one of the root causes of racism; whereas this fact must be fully recognised, particularly in school curricula, C. regretting the fact that the US and Israeli delegations walked out of the conference, which fortunately concluded with the adoption of a declaration and a programme of action, D. regretting the fact that the industrialised countries sent relatively low-ranking representatives, thus demonstrating their lack of interest in the conference, E. having regard to the large number of NGOs that attended the World Conference and the preparatory conferences and to the value of the work done by them, F. deploring the fact that some outrageous statements were made and that there were some highly regrettably outbursts; whereas, however, this cannot be deemed to detract from the importance of the demands made by the NGOs, 1.
