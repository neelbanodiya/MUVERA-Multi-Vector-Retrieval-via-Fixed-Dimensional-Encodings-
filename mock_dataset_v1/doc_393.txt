In the framework of a regulatory dialogue called ‘International Cooperation on Cosmetic Regulation’ (ICCR), the EU, the United States, Japan and Canada agreed in their meeting in July 2008 on cooperation at scientific level with the adoption of the framework for ‘International Cooperation on Alternative Test Methods’ (ICATM). Therefore, the Commission is confident that the provisions of the Cosmetics Directive, which are based on animal welfare protection, are World Trade Organisation compliant and will not be attacked by its counterparts.
