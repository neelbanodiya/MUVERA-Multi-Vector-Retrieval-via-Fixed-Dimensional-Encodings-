In the second round of voting Mr Diamandouros received 294 votes out of 535 Members who voted. The British candidate and current MEP Roy Perry was the only other candidate in the second round and he received 215 votes. The vote was held by secret ballot on the basis of a majority of the votes cast. Speaking after the vote, Roy Perry called for the Code of Good Administrative Behaviour to be adopted swiftly by all the EU institutions. Both the Deputy Foreign Minister of Greece, Mr Yannitsis and Commissioner Chris Patten paid tribute to Mr Diamandouros stating " Greece's loss is Europe's gain!" The responsibilities of the European Ombudsman are considerable. It is by means of the European Ombudsman that European citizens are able to gain redress when examples of maladministration occur within the European Union Institutions or bodies. Mr Diamandouros will replace the current incumbent of the office of Ombudsman, Mr Jacob SÖDERMAN who is retiring. Mr Söderman was elected the first European Ombudsman in June 1995 and re-elected in 1999. Mr Diamandouros will serve until the end of the current parliamentary term in 2004 and one of his first duties will be to take an oath before the European Court of Justice.
