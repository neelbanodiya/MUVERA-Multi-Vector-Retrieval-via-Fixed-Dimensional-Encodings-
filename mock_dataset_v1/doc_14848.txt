Question for written answer E-4251/2010 to the Council Rule 117 Anna Záborská (PPE) (17 June 2010) Subject: Divorce and legal separation — definition of marriage In the context of the negotiations on and the adoption and entry into force of the Council regulation implementing enhanced cooperation in the area of the law applicable to divorce and legal separation (COM(2010)0104 — 2010/0067(CNS)), the Commission issued the following clarification: ‘The proposed regulation will have no bearing on the right of the Member States to define marriage’. Could the Council list the measures taken in respect of each Member State in order to guarantee that no provision of that regulation has a direct or indirect bearing on the right of the Member States to define marriage? What procedure is used in the event of a failure to respect the right of the Member States to define marriage, and which institution takes the final decision determining whether that right has been fully respected? In the context of the legislative and policy proposals which the Council may put forward in the future, what procedure and legal basis should apply when it comes to defining marriage?
