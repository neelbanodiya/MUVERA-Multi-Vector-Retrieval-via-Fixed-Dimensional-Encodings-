Question for written answer E-010981/2011 to the Commission Rule 117 Sergio Paolo Frances Silvestris (PPE) (25 November 2011) Subject: Lizzano Vergine landfill site The Vergine landfill site in Lizzano was sealed off a few days ago because inspections during the course of the year had revealed that there had been management irregularities and a drain had been built for exposed hazardous toxic waste. ‘Attiva Lizzano’, a group working on the ground to protect public health, is calling for all the necessary checks to be carried out to determine the state of the area concerned, including core drilling of cells, whether in use or disused, leachate and groundwater analysis, and monitoring of gaseous emissions. — Are there any European regulations requiring regional authorities to monitor the environmental impact all the time in areas where landfill sites are located? — Are there any rules requiring regional authorities to carry out the checks necessary to protect public health? — If the answer to the preceding questions is yes, can the regional authorities concerned in this instance be made to ensure compliance with the European regulations applicable to the Vergine landfill site?
