Parliament also repeats its view that pluralism in broadcasting is an important safeguard for democracy, pluralism and cultural diversity. MEPs are concerned about the integrity of the media, which they feel is threatened by growing concentration in the industry. Several amendments were adopted calling for rules to be laid down on ownership of television media so as to ensure freedom and pluralism. In addition, the Commission is urged to monitor levels of media concentration in Europe and to draft an updated Green Paper on this issue by the beginning of 2004. MEPs express their deepest sympathy for and solidarity with the families of those who died and with the inhabitants of the devastated areas. The House also salutes the dedication of the full-time and voluntary firemen and members of the public who fought the fires. Parliament notes the decision taken by the Commission to propose the mobilisation of the European Union’s Solidarity Fund with a view to granting aid of €31million to Portugal to enable it to restore its infrastructures and reimburse the cost of the emergency measures, but considers that the contribution from the Solidarity Fund should be nearer the total emergency aid available under the EUSF, assessed at €90m.
