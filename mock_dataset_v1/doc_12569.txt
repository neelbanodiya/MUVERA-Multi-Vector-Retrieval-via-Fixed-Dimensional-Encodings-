P5_TA(2002)0306 Balanced rights between individual Members and Groups (amendment of Rules of Procedure) A5-0252/2000 European Parliament decision amending Parliament's Rules of Procedure to ensure balanced rights between individual Members and Groups (1999/2181(REG)) The European Parliament , – having regard to the proposed amendments to its Rules of Procedure ( B5-0059/1999 and B5-0060/1999 ), – having regard to Rule 181 of its Rules of Procedure, – having regard to its adoption on 14 September 1999 OJ C 54, 25.2.2000, p. 22. of the interpretation of Rule 29 of the Rules of Procedure proposed by the Committee on Constitutional Affairs, – having regard to the order delivered by the President of the Court of First Instance on 25 November 1999 in Case No T-222/99R, – having regard to the report of the Committee on Constitutional Affairs ( A5-0252/2000 ), 1. Decides to amend its Rules of Procedure as indicated; 2. Instructs the Bureau, pursuant to its powers under Rule 22(3), and the Sessional Service to ensure that amendments, requests for split votes, separate votes and roll-call votes made by Members 'on behalf of the political groups' are indeed on behalf of the group and not the individual Member; 3. Instructs its President to forward this decision to the Council and Commission for information. Former text New text Amendment 3 Rule 29, paragraph 1, interpretation (new) Parliament need not normally evaluate the political affinity of members of a Group. In forming a Group together under this Rule, Members concerned accept by definition that they have political affinity. Only when this is denied by the Members concerned is it necessary for Parliament to evaluate whether the Group has been constituted in conformity with the Rules.
