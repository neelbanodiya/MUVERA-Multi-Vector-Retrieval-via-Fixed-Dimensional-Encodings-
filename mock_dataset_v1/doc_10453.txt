Question for written answer E-008709/2011 to the Commission Rule 117 Jens Geier (S&D) (4 October 2011) Subject: Ignalina nuclear power plant Ignalina nuclear power plant (Ignalina NPP) in Lithuania was officially shut down on 31 December 2009 (Reactor Units I and II). What progress has been made so far in decommissioning and dismantling the plant? What costs are being incurred by the closure and dismantling of Ignalina NPP, or are there any cost increases? What is the timeframe for decommissioning the plant, or have there been any delays? What are the reasons for possible cost increases and/or delays? When it acceded to the EU, Lithuania undertook to shut down and decommission Ignalina NPP. What control mechanisms is the Commission putting in place to check and monitor this process and to prevent delays in the decommissioning of the plant? What assurance is there that the decommissioning is being carried out in the best possible way financially and technically? What companies are involved in the closure and decommissioning of Ignalina NPP, and how is the cooperation with the Lithuanian authorities progressing?
