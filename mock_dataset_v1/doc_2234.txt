As a result, most Member States have recently decided to freeze or suspend previously granted arms export licences to destinations currently affected by the unrest. Some of the EU Member States have also formally undertaken a review of their arms export policies, in cooperation with their national parliaments. These developments are also likely to play a role in the upcoming review of the 2008 common position, the implementation of which should be assessed and reviewed by EU Member States three years after its entry into force, i.e. starting December 2011.
