Rapporteur Giacomo SANTINI (EPP-ED, I) for the Justice and Home Affairs Committee is tabling an amendment stating that the programme will, particularly but not exclusively, be intended for third countries that have signed an agreement for the readmission of illegal immigrants or persons whose asylum applications have been denied. Through proposed amendments, the rapporteur aims to clarify the objectives of the programme: to develop legal migration to the EU, in accordance with the economic and social situation in the countries of origin and the host countries, and in accordance with the host countries' reception capacity (11); to help third countries develop legislation with regard to illegal migration, trafficking in human beings and the smuggling of migrants (12); the readmission of illegal immigrants and persons who have unsuccessfully undergone an asylum procedure in the EU (13); to provide information and legal advice on the consequences of illegal immigration (including trafficking in human beings and the smuggling of migrants) and of clandestine employment in the EU; to provide information and legal advice on the possibilities of working legally in the European Union (16); to improve third countries' capacities to implement border controls, including by means of operational co-operation (20). Stepping up the fight against corruption Francesco RUTELLI (ELDR, I) Report on the communication from the Commission to the Council, the European Parliament and the European Economic and Social Committee - On a Comprehensive EU Policy Against Corruption ( COM(2003) 317 - 2003/2154(INI)) Doc.: A5-0367/2003 Procedure : Own-initiative report Debate : 04.12.2003 Francesco RUTELLI (ELDR, I), for the Citizens' Rights Committee, will be tabling a draft own-initiative resolution in response to a Commission paper on EU policy to fight corruption. The rapporteur stresses that the entry into force of the European Arrest Warrant on 1 January 2004 highlights the need for an EU definition of corruption and his committee makes a series of recommendations for tackling the problem in general. MEPs believe, like the Commission, that all Member States should join the Council of Europe's GRECO (Group of states against corruption) group. In addition, an independent evaluation system should be set up to assess the proper implementation by the Member States of anti-corruption legislation.
