Expects the signatory States to honour their commitment to help improve the situation of indigenous peoples, particularly on the labour market; 11. Expects the EU Member States to take action to honour the commitment to promote and protect fully and effectively the human rights and fundamental freedoms of all migrants by ensuring equal treatment in respect of economic, social and cultural rights and recognising civil and political rights, particularly the right to vote; 12. Calls on the Member States to step up implementation of practical measures to combat racism: the adoption or strengthening of anti-racist laws, to be based on the principle that 'racism is a crime'; information and awareness-raising campaigns in the media and educational establishments; training programmes aimed at combating intolerance, particularly among the police and the judiciary; legal action against parties or groups with a racist or xenophobic ideology; and gender mainstreaming in all action to combat racism; 13. Deplores the fact that the final declaration does not call for the abolition of the death penalty and does not cover all forms of discrimination, particularly those based on sexual preference and on caste; 14. Calls on the Member States to adopt law or regulations at the earliest opportunity with a view to implementing the final declaration's recommendations and the plan of action, with the close involvement of organisations representing civil society; 15. Calls for an increase in the budget headings covering action to combat racism and all forms of discrimination; 16. Instructs its President to forward this resolution to the Council, the Commission, the governments and parliaments of the Member States and the candidate countries, the Council of Europe and the Secretary-General of the United Nations.
