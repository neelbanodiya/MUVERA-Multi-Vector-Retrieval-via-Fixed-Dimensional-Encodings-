Question for written answer E-010094/2011 to the Commission Rule 117 Ulrike Rodust (S&D) (7 November 2011) Subject: Definition of ‘most deprived persons’ Article 1(3) of the implementing regulation for the ‘most deprived food aid’ scheme (Regulation (EC) No 807/2010) defines ‘most deprived persons’ as ‘physical persons, whether individuals, families or groups composed of such persons, whose social and financial dependence is recorded or recognised on the basis of eligibility criteria adopted by the competent authorities, or is judged to be so on the basis of the criteria used by charitable organisations and which are approved by the competent authorities’. 1. Will the Commission please state the criteria used in those Member States participating in the scheme to determine who the most deprived persons are? 2. What differences are there between the criteria determined by the Member States? 3. In which Member States are the criteria determined by charitable institutions? Are there different criteria in Member States where a number of institutions are participating in the programme, and are there differences between these and the criteria determined by the competent authorities? 4. What form does the aid provided for the most deprived persons take?
