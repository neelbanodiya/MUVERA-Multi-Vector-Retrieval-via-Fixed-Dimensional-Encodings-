WRITTEN QUESTION E-0213/06 by Werner Langen (PPE‑DE) to the Commission (26 January 2006) Subject: Proposal of an FFH area at Hahn Airport, Rhineland-Palatinate (Germany) According to press reports, the Commission has called for an FFH area to be proposed near the former military airport at Hahn in the Hunsrück, Germany. Hahn Airport is currently one of the most successful regional airports in the whole of Europe. With around 3.5 million passengers in 2005, the airport has now reached a size where further investment is required. According to media reports, the Commission has called for the barbastelle bat habitats in the Ahringsbach valley to be proposed by 19 February 2006. 1. Does the Commission have a list of proposed FFH sites in the German federal state of Rhineland‑Palatinate? What area do these sites cover and where are there still problems? 2. What is the situation regarding the case of the barbastelle bat in the Ahringsbach valley? 3. What further steps does the Commission intend to take? What sanctions could be imposed, if necessary, and under what circumstances?
