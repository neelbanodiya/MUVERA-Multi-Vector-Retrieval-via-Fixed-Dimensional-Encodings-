"Mandatory solidarity" between countries At the insistence of Mr Deprez the principle of "mandatory solidarity" was inserted in the text. This means that all EU countries would be obliged to help in the deployment of the specially trained guards if Frontex requested them to do so. Their mandate would be to act only if a country faces "urgent and exceptional pressure" on its frontiers. This point is particularly relevant to the countries in the Schengen area who have abolished internal border controls - so illegal immigrants entering the Schengen area could in theory travel unchecked. The resolution notes: "border control is in the interest not only of the Member State at whose external borders it is carried out but of all Member States which have abolished internal border control".
