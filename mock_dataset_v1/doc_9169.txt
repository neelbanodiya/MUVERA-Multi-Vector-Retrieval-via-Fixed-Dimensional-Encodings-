Asylum MEPs condemn the delays in adopting the basic instruments of a common asylum and immigration policy. They call on the Member States to keep detention of asylum-seekers to a minimum and restrict it solely to exceptional cases that meet UNHCR guidelines. The House calls on the Council to adopt the draft directive providing for secondary protection to people not covered by the Geneva Convention but unable to return to their country of origin. It voices concern at the large number of people who lost their lives in 2002 whilst seeking refuge in the EU and condemns the serious situation faced by unaccompanied minors seeking asylum. It argues that people should not be extradited to countries where they might face the death penalty or torture. Discrimination The Parliament calls on the Member States to guarantee that all children on their territory will be guaranteed access to education regardless of their familyâ€™s administrative situation. Similarly, any person living on their territory should be granted access to health care. In addition, Member States should relax their naturalisation procedures, ensuring that residents of foreign origin can obtain citizenship if they so wish. MEPs want the Member States to abolish all forms of discrimination against homosexuals, in particular as regards the right to marry and adopt children . And non-marital couples should be able benefit from the rules on freedom of movement within the EU. The Parliament wants that long-term residents (three years) who are third-country nationals should be entitled to take part in local elections and elections to the European Parliament. Among other concerns voiced in the report are transparency and the right of access to documents, equality between men and women and the rights of the elderly and people with disabilities.
