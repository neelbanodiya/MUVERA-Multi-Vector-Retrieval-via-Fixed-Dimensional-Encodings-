Answer given by Mr Piebalgs on behalf of the Commission (4 June 2008) The aim of the investment project is to increase the electricity generating capacity of Slovenské Elektrárne (‘SE’) through the completion of Units 3 and 4 of Mochovce Nuclear Power Plant, Slovak Republic. Slovenské Elektrárne contends that the new plant will contribute mainly to meeting additional regional demand for electricity and also Community wide. In accordance with Article 41 of the Euratom Treaty, Slovenské Elektrárne communicated to the Commission an investment project for the completion of Units 3 and 4 of Mochovce Nuclear Power Plant in Slovakia. The communication was received on 20 July 2007. By means of an internal Commission working group and discussions between the Commission and the Investor as provided for in Article 43 of the Euratom Treaty, the Commission is assessing all aspects of the investment which relate to the objectives of the Euratom Treaty pursuant to Council Regulation (Euratom) No 2587/1999 of 2 December 1999 defining the investment projects to be communicated to the Commission in accordance with Article 41 of the Treaty establishing the Euratom Atomic Energy Community OJ L 315, 9.12.1999. and Commission Regulation (EC) No 1209/2000 determining procedures for effecting the commu­nications prescribed under Article 41 of the Treaty establishing the European Atomic Energy Community OJ L 138, 9.6.2000. (Euratom). As soon as the Commission considers that the information gathered during these extensive discussions is sufficient to establish its opinion on the investment, the Commission Point of view under Article 43 will be adopted.
