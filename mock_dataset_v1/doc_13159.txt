15 December 1999 JOINT MOTION FOR A RESOLUTION pursuant to Rule 37(4) the Rules of Procedure by the following Members: Poettering, Oostlander, Morillon and van Velzen, on behalf of the PPE/DE Group Barón Crespo and Hänsch, on behalf of the PSE Group Duff and Cox, on behalf of the ELDR Group Hautala, Lannoye and Maes, on behalf of the Verts/ALE Group replacing motions by: - the ELDR Group ( B5-0327/1999 ) - the PPE/DE Group ( B5-0353/1999 ) - the Verts/ALE Group ( B5-0354/1999 ) - the PSE Group ( B5-0357/1999 ) on the Helsinki European Council The European Parliament, - having regard to the conclusions of the Luxembourg, Berlin and Cologne European Councils and having heard the statements by the President of the Council and the President of the Commission on the conclusions of the Helsinki European Council, - having regard to its resolution of 19 November 1997 on the Treaty of Amsterdam OJ C 371, 8.12.1997, p. 99. , - having regard to its resolution of 18 November 1999 on the preparation of the reform of the Treaties and the next Intergovernmental Conference Texts adopted at that sitting, point 4. , - having regard to its resolution of 2 December 1999 on the preparation of the meeting of the European Council in Helsinki on 10 and 11 December 1999 Texts adopted at that sitting, point 13. , - having regard to its resolution of 16 September 1999 on the establishment of the Charter of Fundamental Rights, - having regard to the conclusions of the Helsinki European Council, On enlargement 1. Welcomes the Council's decision to begin negotiations on accession with Romania, Slovakia, Latvia, Lithuania, Bulgaria and Malta, thus creating a fully flexible, multi-speed accession process, in which these countries are given the possibility to catch up within a reasonable period of time with those candidate countries already in negotiations, if they have made sufficient progress in their preparations; 2. Urges the Commission and the Council to pay particular attention in the implementation of the pre-accession strategies and during the negotiations to strengthening the rule of law, social progress in the applicant countries, protection of the environment, energy safety and security, protection of minorities, gender discrimination, and asylum and migration policy; 3.
